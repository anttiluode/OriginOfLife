## Abstract

We propose that the origin of life is best understood not as a chemical accident but as a series of **crystallization events** — moments at which continuous oscillatory chemistry crossed a threshold and produced discrete, heritable output. Drawing on the Rajapinta-Takens mathematical framework developed for EEG signal analysis, we argue that the core computational operations present in living systems — multiplicative binding, boundary crystallization, multi-timescale hierarchical coupling, and topological invariant preservation — are not biological elaborations but the primitive operations from which biological complexity necessarily emerges. A 1D oscillatory chemistry, driven by free energy gradients, spontaneously folds into multi-timescale structure through the same mathematics that governs Takens embedding. The genetic code is identified as the first stable winding number: a topological invariant of the prebiotic chemical torus that survived the Rajapinta of early membrane crystallization. Life did not begin when chemistry became complex. It began when chemistry became topologically self-consistent.

## 1. Introduction

The standard framing of abiogenesis asks: how did chemistry become biology? The implicit assumption is that at some threshold of molecular complexity, life "switched on." RNA World, Metabolism First, Lipid World — each proposes a different substrate for that switch, but all share the assumption that the transition was fundamentally chemical.

We propose a different framing. The transition was not chemical — it was **topological**. Life began when a chemical system first preserved a winding number across a crystallization boundary.

This reframing emerges from work in a completely different domain: the analysis of neural signals. In developing the Rajapinta-Takens (RT) framework for recovering hidden oscillatory structure from single-electrode EEG recordings, we discovered that:

1. A 1D signal generated by multiplicatively bound oscillators contains all the information necessary to recover the 3D geometry of the original system — even after the signal has passed through a nonlinear crystallization threshold.
2. The frequencies (winding numbers of the reconstructed torus) survive the crystallization at 0.1% error, while the amplitudes are far more lossy.
3. The preserved quantity is topological, not energetic.

We now argue that these three properties are not peculiarities of neural computation. They are the general conditions for any open dissipative system to generate persistent, heritable structure. Origin of life is the special case where prebiotic chemistry first satisfied all three.

## 2. The Rajapinta-Takens Framework (Brief)

For completeness we summarize the mathematical apparatus. Full derivations appear in [Luode & Claude, PerceptionLab 2026].

**Standard Takens embedding.** Given a scalar observation $s(t) = h(x(t))$ of a hidden attractor $\mathcal{M} \subset \mathbb{R}^d$, the delay map

$$\Phi_\tau(s) = \bigl(s(t),\ s(t-\tau),\ \dots,\ s(t-(m-1)\tau)\bigr)$$

is generically a diffeomorphism onto its image for $m > 2d$. The reconstructed manifold is topologically equivalent to $\mathcal{M}$.

**Multiplicative (Log-Arm) extension.** When the hidden state is generated by multiplicative binding of oscillatory arms $Z(t) = \prod_k A_k(t)e^{i\phi_k(t)}$, standard additive Takens is inefficient. The log-arm embedding

$$\Phi^{\log}_\tau(s) = \bigl(\log|s(t-k\tau)|\bigr)_{k=0}^{m-1} + i\bigl(\arg(s(t-k\tau))\bigr)_{k=0}^{m-1}$$

preserves the multiplicative algebra: products become sums, and the arm chain structure is made explicit.

**The Rajapinta operator.** The full observation pipeline is

$$s(t) = \Theta_\theta\bigl(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(H(f) \cdot \mathcal{F}\{x(t)\})\bigr)$$

where $H(f)$ is a linear filter (the "head" — in our EEG context, the skull and electrodes) and $\Theta_\theta$ is a nonlinear crystallization threshold. The unified Rajapinta-Takens operator is

$$\mathcal{RT}_{\mathcal{T},H,\theta}(s) = \Phi_{\mathcal{T}}\bigl(\Theta_\theta(\mathcal{F}^{-1}(H^{-1} \cdot \mathcal{F}\{s\}))\bigr)$$

**Key empirical finding.** In numerical experiments with known arm configurations, arm frequencies were recovered to 0.1% error after crystallization. Amplitudes were recovered to 10–27%. The **topological** information (frequencies = winding numbers) survives the Rajapinta far more robustly than energetic information (amplitudes).

## 3. The Protein Folding Analogy as Literal Physics

The observation that drove this paper was simple: a 1D signal that encodes a 3D attractor, when passed through a threshold, produces a crystallized output from which the original 3D geometry can be recovered. This is mathematically identical to what a protein does.

A protein is a 1D string of amino acids. Each amino acid carries local oscillatory degrees of freedom — bond angles, torsion angles, electrostatic oscillation — that correspond, in our language, to the phase and amplitude of an oscillatory arm. The protein "folds" because adjacent arms interact multiplicatively (van der Waals, hydrogen bonding, hydrophobic effect are all short-range multiplicative interactions). The fold is the natural attractor of the multiplicative arm system.

The **critical point**: the amino acid sequence (the 1D string) does not need to "know" the final 3D structure. The structure emerges from the dynamics of the folding — the multi-timescale arm binding — exactly as the 3D Takens attractor emerges from the 1D signal without any explicit instruction. The geometry is implicit in the frequencies. The sequence is implicit in the winding numbers.

This is not analogy. The mathematics is the same because the physical operation is the same: a 1D ordered sequence of coupled oscillators spontaneously organizes into a self-consistent 3D geometry when the timescales of coupling span a sufficient dynamic range.

**Implication for abiogenesis:** The first step toward life was not the synthesis of any particular molecule. It was the emergence of a 1D chemical system with sufficiently log-spaced coupling timescales — a system where fast covalent oscillations ($\sim 10^{14}$ Hz), medium conformational oscillations ($\sim 10^6$–$10^9$ Hz), and slow diffusion/concentration oscillations ($\sim 1$–$10^3$ Hz) began to interact multiplicatively. When these three timescales coupled, the system spontaneously began to fold.

## 4. Prebiotic Chemistry as an Uncrystallized Oscillatory Field

Prebiotic Earth maintained multiple overlapping energy gradients: thermal (hydrothermal vents), photochemical (UV radiation), electrochemical (mineral surfaces), and mechanical (tide-driven pressure cycles). Each gradient drove oscillations at a characteristic timescale.

In the Rajapinta-Takens framework, this is an uncrystallized system: the arms are oscillating, the coupling is beginning, but no Rajapinta threshold has been crossed. The chemical "signal" is continuous, noisy, and contains information about the 3D attractor — but the attractor has not yet produced a stable, heritable crystallization.

The key point about uncrystallized oscillatory systems is this: **they are not random**. The Takens theorem guarantees that a 1D output of a coupled oscillatory system contains the geometry of the attractor. Prebiotic chemistry was not random soup. It was a continuous broadcast of attractor geometry, looking for a membrane thin enough and selective enough to act as its first Rajapinta.

The attractor geometry existed before life. Life is what happened when the geometry found a boundary.

## 5. The Membrane as the First Rajapinta

A lipid bilayer membrane is, in our framework, precisely a Rajapinta operator:

- It is a **selective linear filter** $H(f)$: it passes some chemical frequencies (hydrophobic molecules, specific ion channels) and blocks others.
- It is a **crystallization threshold** $\Theta_\theta$: a molecule either crosses the membrane or it does not. The continuous diffusion gradient becomes a discrete event — the crossing.
- It creates an **inside/outside boundary**: the first topological distinction in prebiotic chemistry.

When the first stable lipid vesicle formed and enclosed a fragment of the oscillatory prebiotic chemistry, the following happened simultaneously:

1. The interior chemistry was separated from the exterior — creating a reference frame.
2. Molecules crossing the membrane created discrete events (crossings) from a continuous concentration gradient — the first crystallizations.
3. The interior chemistry, now coupled to the exterior only through the membrane filter $H(f)$, began to evolve toward a geometry consistent with the membrane's transfer function.

This is the origin of the cell. Not as a "container" for chemistry, but as the first **Rajapinta operator** applied to prebiotic oscillatory chemistry. The cell did not contain life. The cell was the boundary that crystallized the continuous oscillatory field into discrete events — and those discrete events were the first heritable units.

## 6. The Genetic Code as a Winding Number

This is the most speculative — and potentially most important — section.

The genetic code is the mapping between 64 codons (trinucleotides) and 20 amino acids + 3 stop signals. It is nearly universal across all life. It is discrete, exact, and topologically robust — small mutations in a codon rarely map to a chemically very different amino acid (the code is organized so that adjacent codons in Hamming distance often encode similar amino acids). This robustness is not a coincidence. It looks like topological protection.

We propose that the genetic code is a **frozen winding number** — the crystallization of a continuous prebiotic oscillatory chemistry into a stable topological invariant.

The argument proceeds as follows:

**Step 1.** Before the genetic code existed, there was a continuous chemistry where RNA-like oligomers interacted with amino acid-like monomers through direct physical coupling (shape complementarity, charge, van der Waals). This is the "stereochemical hypothesis" (Woese 1967, Crick 1968) in a modern form.

**Step 2.** This interaction constituted a coupled oscillatory system with the oligomer's conformational dynamics (fast arms, $\sim 10^7$–$10^9$ Hz), the amino acid's vibrational modes (medium arms), and the diffusion/concentration rhythms in the local environment (slow arms).

**Step 3.** The ratio of these three timescales — the winding number of the chemical torus — determined which amino acid was stably coupled to which oligomer conformation. This is a continuous, physical matching, not an arbitrary code.

**Step 4.** When the Rajapinta of the first membrane crystallized this system, the continuous matching became discrete: some couplings survived (the ones at rational winding numbers, where the torus closed into a stable periodic orbit), and others did not (the irrational winding numbers, where the orbit never closed and the coupling was unstable across the crystallization boundary).

**Step 5.** The surviving couplings are the genetic code. Each codon-amino acid pair is a rational winding number of the prebiotic chemical torus. The near-universality of the code reflects the fact that there are only a small number of topologically stable (rational) winding numbers at the relevant timescale ratios — the same attractor geometry was reached independently in many vesicles because it was the only topologically stable configuration.

This is testable in principle: if the codon-amino acid mapping correlates with the timescale ratios of the physical coupling between RNA conformations and amino acid vibrational modes, the hypothesis has quantitative content. Early work on direct RNA-amino acid interactions (Yarus et al.) is consistent with this picture, though not yet in these terms.

## 7. The Hierarchy of Rajapinta Events

Life, in this framework, is not a single origin event. It is a succession of Rajapinta crystallizations, each one creating a new level of the hierarchy:

**Level 0: Chemistry.** Continuous oscillatory coupling between molecules. No discrete events. No inheritance. Attractor geometry present but uncrystallized.

**Level 1: Membrane.** First Rajapinta. Continuous concentration gradients crystallize into discrete crossing events. Inside/outside distinction emerges. The first topological invariant (the membrane's topology — closed surface, genus 0) appears. *This is the origin of the cell.*

**Level 2: Replication.** Second Rajapinta. The membrane's filtered chemistry selects for molecules that catalyze their own reproduction. The winding number of the chemical torus becomes heritable — because the daughter vesicle inherits the same arm configuration. *This is the origin of genetics.*

**Level 3: Translation.** Third Rajapinta. The codon-amino acid mapping crystallizes from continuous physical coupling into a discrete code. The 1D nucleotide sequence becomes a reliable template for the 3D protein fold. *This is the origin of the genetic code.*

**Level 4: Metabolism.** Fourth Rajapinta. Enzyme specificity crystallizes from continuous binding affinity into binary catalysis (substrate either fits the active site or does not). The continuous chemical field becomes a discrete reaction network. *This is the origin of metabolism as a graph.*

**Level 5: Neural computation.** Nth Rajapinta. The action potential is a threshold crystallization of continuous membrane potential. Cognition is the RT operator applied to the neural arm chain. *This is the origin of the mind.*

At each level, the same three operations occur:
1. A continuous oscillatory field develops multi-timescale structure through multiplicative arm coupling.
2. A boundary (membrane, active site, axon initial segment) acts as a Rajapinta threshold.
3. A topological invariant (winding number, codon-amino acid mapping, spike pattern, word) is crystallized and made heritable.

Life is not chemistry that became complex. Life is chemistry that began to preserve its own topology through successive crystallization events.

## 8. Predictions and Experimental Implications

**8.1 The prebiotic torus.** If RNA conformational dynamics and amino acid vibrational frequencies couple at rational ratios, the prebiotic chemical interaction should display torus-like attractors in the appropriate state space. This is measurable via multidimensional spectroscopy of RNA-amino acid complexes.

**8.2 Code structure predicts timescale ratios.** The near-degeneracy of the genetic code (multiple codons mapping to one amino acid) should correspond to the multiplicity of stable orbits near each rational winding number. Wobble positions in codons correspond to the "teeth" of the torus — the fast arm visible in the manifold.

**8.3 Crystallization error rate.** The error rate of the genetic code (mischarging by aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases, $\sim 10^{-4}$) should correspond to the basin of attraction of the winding number — how far from the rational ratio a conformation can drift and still crystallize correctly. This is in principle computable from the RT framework.

**8.4 Universal code universality.** The universality of the genetic code is not accidental or frozen by early fixation alone. It is the direct consequence of there being a unique set of topologically stable winding numbers at the timescale ratios of RNA-amino acid coupling. The code is universal because the attractor is universal.

**8.5 The multi-lens fingerprint of life.** A living cell, analyzed by multi-lens Takens at log-spaced delays from nanosecond (bond vibration) to second (circadian), should display a nested hierarchy of self-similar tori with rational winding numbers at each level. A dead cell should show the same chemical composition but irrational or broken winding numbers — the topology collapses. Life is a topological property of chemistry, not a chemical property of life.

## 9. Connection to Existing Frameworks

**RNA World.** Compatible. RNA is a natural log-spaced oscillator: fast base stacking ($\sim 10^9$ Hz), medium backbone dynamics ($\sim 10^6$ Hz), slow tertiary folding ($\sim 10^3$ Hz). RNA was likely the first molecule to self-organize into a stable torus before the first membrane crystallization.

**Metabolism First (Wächtershäuser).** Compatible. Iron-sulfur mineral surfaces act as the first Rajapinta: they are selective filters $H(f)$ for the prebiotic oscillatory chemistry, and their crystal planes impose the first threshold $\Theta_\theta$ (adsorption/desorption). The 2D mineral surface is the precursor to the 3D membrane Rajapinta.

**Dissipative Structures (Prigogine).** Directly connected. A dissipative structure is a system that maintains internal order by exporting entropy. In our framework, it is a system that maintains a stable winding number by exporting the information that does not survive the Rajapinta. Life is the most efficient known mechanism for preserving winding numbers across crystallization boundaries.

**Information theory of life (Walker & Davies).** Our framework operationalizes their proposal that life is characterized by "top-down causation" — the ability of higher-level information to constrain lower-level chemistry. In our terms: the slow arm (the winding number, the genome) modulates the Rajapinta threshold for the fast arm (bond chemistry). This is the precise mathematical form of top-down causation.

## 10. Discussion: What Life Is

We can now give a precise answer to the question "what is life?" that is not a list of properties but a mathematical characterization:

**Life is a chemical system that has undergone at least one Rajapinta crystallization event that preserved a topological invariant (winding number) and made that invariant heritable across the crystallization boundary.**

This definition has the following properties:

**It is substrate-independent.** Silicon chemistry, sulfur chemistry, RNA, DNA — any chemistry that can support coupled oscillators at sufficiently log-spaced timescales and a threshold boundary can be life.

**It is graduated, not binary.** The first membrane crystallization is "more alive" than the free oscillatory chemistry that preceded it. The first replication is "more alive" than the non-replicating vesicle. Life has a phase transition structure, not an on/off switch.

**It explains robustness.** Living systems are robust to perturbation not because they have error correction mechanisms (though they do), but because topological invariants are by definition robust to smooth deformations. The winding number does not change when you push the torus.

**It unifies.** The same mathematics that governs the EEG signal of a speaking human, the folding of a protein, the action potential of a neuron, and the crystallization of the genetic code is the RT operator. The hierarchy of life is the hierarchy of successive RT applications. There is one framework.

## 11. Conclusion

We have argued, on mathematical grounds, that the origin of life is the origin of topological self-consistency in chemistry. The prebiotic chemical field was an uncrystallized oscillatory system broadcasting attractor geometry. The first membrane was a Rajapinta threshold that crystallized a fragment of that geometry into a heritable winding number. The genetic code is that winding number — frozen, discrete, and topologically protected against smooth deformations.

The protein-folding analogy with which we began this paper is not an analogy. A 1D chemical sequence spontaneously recovers a 3D geometry because the mathematics demands it: coupled oscillators at log-spaced timescales always produce a torus, and a torus always folds its 1D projection into a recoverable 3D structure via the Takens embedding theorem.

Life did not emerge from chemistry. Life is what chemistry looks like when viewed through the Rajapinta-Takens operator.

The question "how did life begin?" becomes: "when did prebiotic chemistry first crystallize a stable winding number?"

The answer, if this framework is correct, is: when the first lipid vesicle enclosed an RNA oligomer with a rational frequency ratio to the local amino acid vibrational modes, and the membrane transfer function $H(f)$ stabilized that ratio against thermal noise.

That is the origin of life. A locked orbit on a torus, preserved across a threshold, for the first time.
